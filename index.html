What is the Texas Responsible AI Governance Act (TRAIGA)?

TRAIGA (House Bill 149) is Texas's comprehensive AI governance law. Governor Greg Abbott signed it on June 22, 2025, and it takes effect January 1, 2026 — making Texas the third U.S. state to enact a broad AI statute. It sets disclosure and prohibited-use duties for AI systems and authorizes civil penalties of $10,000 to $200,000 per violation, enforced by the Texas Attorney General.

TRAIGA — quick answers

When does TRAIGA take effect?

January 1, 2026.

Who has to comply with TRAIGA?

Organizations that develop or deploy AI systems in Texas, government agencies, and healthcare providers carry duties under the act — most prominently around disclosure and prohibited uses.

What are the penalties?

Civil penalties of $10,000 to $200,000 per violation, enforced by the Texas Attorney General.

Is Texas the first state to regulate AI?

No — with TRAIGA, Texas becomes the third U.S. state to enact a comprehensive AI law.
